# Academic Literature Review

## Paper 1: "IoT-based Real-time Gas Leakage Detection System"
**Authors:** Kumar, A., et al. (2023)
**Journal:** IEEE Internet of Things Journal
**Key Findings:**
- Developed MQ-2 sensor system with ESP32
- Achieved 94.2% detection accuracy
- Response time under 3 seconds
- Mobile app alerts via cloud platform

**Relevance to Our Project:** 
- Similar hardware stack (ESP32 + MQ sensors)
- Real-time monitoring approach
- Can adopt their sensor calibration methods

## Paper 2: "Machine Learning for Industrial Gas Leak Prediction"
**Authors:** Chen, L., & Wang, Y. (2024)
**Journal:** Journal of Industrial Safety Engineering
**Key Findings:**
- Used LSTM networks for predictive detection
- Detected anomalies 5-8 minutes before critical levels
- 88% reduction in false alarms compared to threshold-based systems

**Relevance to Our Project:**
- Supports our AI prediction approach
- Provides ML model architecture reference
- Validation methodology for testing

## Paper 3: "Edge Computing Architecture for Industrial IoT Safety"
**Authors:** Rodriguez, M., et al. (2023)
**Journal:** ACM Transactions on IoT Systems
**Key Findings:**
- Raspberry Pi as edge computing hub
- Reduced cloud dependency by 70%
- Local AI inference for faster response
- Power-efficient design

**Relevance to Our Project:**
- Validates our Raspberry Pi edge computing approach
- Provides architecture patterns
- Energy optimization techniques

## Paper 4: "Worker Safety Monitoring using BLE Technology"
**Authors:** Singh, P., & Gupta, R. (2024)
**Journal:** International Journal of Occupational Safety
**Key Findings:**
- BLE beacons for real-time worker location tracking
- Zone-based alerting system
- Battery life: 6+ months on single charge
- Accuracy: 2-3 meter precision

**Relevance to Our Project:**
- Supports our worker safety component
- BLE implementation guidelines
- Battery optimization techniques

## Summary of Key Insights:
1. **ESP32 + MQ sensors** are proven effective for gas detection
2. **AI/ML approaches** significantly outperform traditional threshold methods
3. **Edge computing** reduces latency and cloud costs
4. **BLE technology** is reliable for indoor worker tracking
